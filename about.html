History, Rules & User Menual

History

Tic-tac-toe, also known as noughts and crosses, has a long history that dates back to ancient times. The game is believed to have originated in ancient Egypt around 1300 BCE, where a similar game was played on a grid drawn in the sand. It evolved over time and spread across different cultures. In ancient Rome, a game called "Terni Lapilli," played on a grid, had similar rules, though players used pebbles instead of X's and O's. The modern version of tic-tac-toe, as we know it today, gained popularity in the 19th century, particularly in the United States and Europe. By the 20th century, tic-tac-toe became a widely recognized children's game, often played on paper or chalkboards. Its simplicity and quick play have made it a timeless pastime, enjoyed by generations across the world. With the advent of digital games, tic-tac-toe found new life in the form of video games, further cementing its place in popular culture.

Rules

The goal of the game is to get three of your marks (either X or O) in a row, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, on a 3x3 grid.

  1. Players: Two players take turns. One player uses X and the other uses O.
  2. Gameplay:
    • The game starts with an empty 3x3 grid.
    • Player 1 places their mark (X) on any empty square.
    • Player 2 places their mark (O) on any empty square.
    • Players continue to take turns until one of the following occurs:
      • One player gets three marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally).
      • All squares are filled, resulting in a draw if no one wins.
  3. Winning: A player wins by placing three of their marks in a row, either:
    • Horizontally (across one of the rows),
    • Vertically (down one of the columns),
    • Diagonally (from one corner to the opposite corner).
  4. Draw: f all squares are filled and no player has three marks in a row, the game is a draw (or "tie").
  5. End of Game: The game ends when either:
    • One player wins,
    • All squares are filled with no winner, resulting in a draw.
